# Notes:
This is the Oriental dialect. Non-Oriental Hebrew has a few less phonemes than the Oriental
dialect. In the JIPA article the phoneme inventory for Oriental Hebrew was provided,
followed by transcriptions in both Oriental and non-Oriental Hebrew. 
Nothing was mentioned in the text about geminate/long consonants. Because many of them are
separated by a stress symbol, they are assumed to belong to different syllables, and I have left in double consonant
symbols as in the original article.
