# Notes:
"Although Hellwig (2011) considers the contrast between the two types of voiceless 
oral stops to be one of aspiration, other students of Goemai have analysed the contrast 
differently. Wolff (1959), for example, labels the contrast as fortis vs. lenis, and Hoffman 
(1975) labels it as glottalized vs. non-glattlized." p83 JIPA article

Goemai has two level tones: high <  ́ > and low < ̀ >. They combine to create HL < ̂ > 
and LH < ̌ >.

Glottal stop is considered non-phonemic in the JIPA illustration, but rather a 
regular phonological process. As with other languages that contain glottal stops in their transcriptions
I have not included it in the phonemic inventory.
