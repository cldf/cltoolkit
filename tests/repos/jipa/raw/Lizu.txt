# Notes:
In the original paper/N/ is used to represent the homorganic nasal in contrastive prenasalized clusters.
Lizu has a "...'culminative' tonal system, in which no more than one pitch pattern is pronounced per word" p81.
Monosyllabic words have two contrastive tones marked at the beginning of the syllable: 
(R) is low rising; and (F) is high falling.  In trisyllabic words there are three possible pitch patterns:
(EP) equally prominant contour; (LP) left-prominent contour; (RP) right-priminent contour.
