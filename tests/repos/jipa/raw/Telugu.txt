# Notes:
In the JIPA paper the authors essentially illustrate that geminate consonants
are phonemes because they are contrastive (minimal pairs provided) with non-geminates.
However, they do not posit that they are phonemes. Since a list of those phonemes that
cannot be geminate is provided, and contrastive minimal pairs have been provided, I include
the geminate forms as separate phonemes here.
