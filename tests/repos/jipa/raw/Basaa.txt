# Notes:
High tone < ́>, low tone < ̀>, rising tone < ̌> and falling < ̂>
In the JIPA article prenasalised stops are indicated by a superscript homorganic 
nasal preceding the voiced stop symbol. I was unable to replicate this, and so have 
just used a superscript <n> preceding the stop.
Nasals can be syllabic, in which case they can take tone.
The click isn't mentioned in the text.
[r] isn't mentioned in the text. Because there are no instances of the voiced tap
in the transcript, it is assumed to represent this sound, either as the tap or an allophone
of it.
