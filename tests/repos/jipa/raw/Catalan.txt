# Additional diacritics in orthography:
Umlaut <v̈> is placed over vowels to indicate a hiatus between the vowel with the umlaut
and the vowel preceding it. 
Umlaut is placed over /u/ to mark that it is not silent in the groups <gü> <qü>.
Umlaut on /u/ is also used to represent /w/.
The acute accent <é, í, ó, ú> is used to mark stressed close vowels.
The grave accent <à, è, ò> is used to mark stressed open vowels.
<ç> is only used before /a, u, o/ to represent /s/.

Orthography used in bible worked out from a combination of the Gospel of Mark,
the JIPA article and Wikipedia https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catalan_orthography#Sound_to_spelling_correspondences
The grapheme is in between angle brackets <>, and the phoneme is between //. 
A hyphen is used to indicate that two graphemes may be used for a single phoneme,
or two phonemes may be represented by a single grapheme.
