# Notes:
There is dispute amongst linguists how many tones there are in Temne.  In this paper the 
authors argue for two tones: High tone represented by diacritic V́ and low tone represented
by diacritic V̀. 

No mention is made of the symbol <y>.  Presumably it is being used as the palatal approximant /j/.


# Errors:
/y/ not in phoneme inventory, but present in transcription. Possibly author’s typo – meant to be palatal approximant.

# Orthography notes:
Sources for working out the phoneme- orthography correspondences were the JIPA article, 
the online bible version http://paralleltext.info/data/tem-x-bible/41/001/,
The Sierra Leone Temne language manual http://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/ED290325.pdf and
OLAC, the Rosetta project https://ia600503.us.archive.org/11/items/rosettaproject_tem_ortho-1/rosettaproject_tem_ortho-1.pdf 
/tʃ/ and /ʃ/ occur in the Toni dialect and are not represented in the orthography. In the JIPA
article the sample text does not contain these sounds, and none of the other sources on Temne
orthography represent them separately. /tʃ/ corresponds to /t/ in other dialects, and /ʃ/ corresponds
to /s/ in other dialects, hence they are written using those graphemes.
Despite being a tone language, no tone is marked in the orthography.
